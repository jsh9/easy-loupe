Compare view displays selected photos side by side so you can choose the
strongest frame from a small set.

## 1. Enter Compare View

Select at least two photos in culling view or browse view, then press `C`.
Compare view displays up to the current compare limit. If your selection is
larger than that limit, EasyLoupe keeps the full selection in memory for when
you return to browse view.

The default limit is 8 photos. Change it from `Compare > Limit`.

## 2. Active Photo

One pane is active. Arrow keys move the active pane. Metadata shortcuts apply
to the active compare photo, not every visible compare pane.

Use:

- `1`-`5` or `0` for rating.
- `6`-`9` or `` ` `` for color label.
- `P`, `X`, or `U` for picked/rejected flag.

## 3. Zoom And Inspection

Press `Z` in the compare grid to toggle focus zoom for the compared panes. Use
`-`, `=`, or `+` to zoom, and `W`, `A`, `S`, `D` to pan the active or locked
panes. The lock button controls whether zoom and pan stay synchronized across
panes.

Press `Space` to open the active photo alone. In that selected-photo view,
press `Space` or `Z` to switch between fit view and 100 percent inspection.

## 4. Leaving Compare View

- Press `Esc` in a selected-photo compare view to return to the compare grid.
- Press `Esc` in the compare grid to return to the previous view.
- Press `G` to return to browse view with the original pre-compare selection.
