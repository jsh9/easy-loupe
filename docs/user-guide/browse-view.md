Browse view shows the loaded folder as a full-photo grid. It is useful for
quick scanning, rough selection, and returning to a specific photo without
moving through the thumbnail strip one item at a time.

## 1. Enter And Leave Browse View

Press `G` from culling view to enter browse view. Press `Space` to return to
culling view for the current photo, or double-click a photo in the grid.

Returning from browse view opens the photo in fit-to-window view. If that photo
has remembered manual zoom state, press `Space` again in culling view to return
to manual zoom for it.

## 2. Selection

Browse view uses normal grid selection gestures. Selecting a photo updates the
current photo, and the culling view stays synchronized in the background.

After scene detection, browse view still shows individual photos rather than
only scene stacks. If you open a photo from a scene, culling view selects the
scene stack on the left and the exact photo in the horizontal scene strip.

## 3. Tagging From Browse View

Metadata shortcuts work in browse view:

- `1`-`5` sets a rating, and `0` clears it.
- `6`-`9` sets red, yellow, green, or blue color labels, and `` ` `` clears the
  color label.
- `P`, `X`, and `U` pick, reject, or clear the flag.

Press `C` to compare the current selection.
